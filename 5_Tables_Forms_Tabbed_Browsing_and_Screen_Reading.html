Overview

The capybara (Hydrochoerus hydrochaeris) is the largest living rodent, native to South America. Capybaras inhabit savannas and dense forests near bodies of water and live in social groups of up to 100 individuals. They are hunted for meat, hide, and grease from their thick skin.

Habitat & Ecology

Capybaras are semiaquatic mammals found throughout South America, except Chile. They are excellent swimmers and can hold their breath underwater for up to five minutes. Many escapees from captivity live in similar watery habitats worldwide, including Florida.

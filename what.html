Definition

Universal Design is an approach to accessibility that ensures products can be used by the widest range of people. Whether designing a website, app, or physical product, the experience of all users should be considered. Universal Design blends accessibility and usability to create solutions that work for everyone—not just a select group.

The Seven Principles

  • Equitable Use
  • Flexibility in Use
  • Simple & Intuitive Use
  • Perceptible Information
  • Tolerance for Error
  • Low Physical Effort
  • Size & Space for Approach and Use

Real-World Web Examples

Examples include responsive layouts, semantic HTML, keyboard accessibility, high contrast, and properly labeled forms that support users with assistive technologies.

Quick Facts

  • Universal Design benefits everyone, not just users with disabilities.
  • The U.S. recognizes 13 disability categories under federal law.
  • Over one billion people worldwide are estimated to have a disability.

Accessibility Reminder

Small decisions—like heading structure, alt text, color contrast, and clear link labels—have a major impact on whether users can successfully interact with a site.
